Features

Built for people who actually count the minutes.

Most "grind calculators" just divide. This one simulates your day, respects cooldowns, tracks passive income accrual between sells, and tells you exactly when each run happens.

01 / SIM

Chronological day sim

Runs are scheduled in order with real cooldowns and setup downtime. If Cayo is on a 144-minute cooldown, you won't see it back in the queue until it's actually ready.

02 / PASSIVE

Passive accrual tracking

Nightclub and acid lab-style passives accumulate stock between sessions. See exactly when the next full sell lands — and what's still pending at day's end.

03 / SEARCH

Deadline ↔ daily hours

Pick a deadline and we binary-search the hours/day you need. Or pick your daily hours and we'll tell you how many days it takes. Either direction.

04 / EDIT

Fully customizable

Payouts, durations, cooldowns, and downtime are all editable. Add your own activities, or tweak the defaults to match your own rig, build, or patch.

05 / LOCAL

Local-first, zero tracking

Everything runs in your browser. Settings persist in localStorage. No account, no analytics, no server — your grind, your business.

06 / OPEN

Single HTML file

The whole tool is one self-contained HTML file. Save it, fork it, host it anywhere. No build step, no dependencies, no drama.

How it works

Three inputs. One honest schedule.

It's intentionally simple. The math underneath isn't — but you don't have to think about that.

01

Pick your activities

Toggle which heists and businesses you actually run. Each has a payout, a duration, a cooldown, and any setup downtime baked in.

02

Set the target

Tell it how much money you need — $10M for that new Kosatka, $50M for the next garage — and when you need it by.

03

Get a day-by-day plan

A full breakdown: which run, in what order, how long, when passives are ready to sell, and what's still pending at the end of each session.

Reads like a mission briefing.

Every day tells you what to do, in order, with real timing — not a blob of "average per hour."

  • Merged runs — three Cayos back-to-back show as one clean line
  • Cooldown gaps — attributed as downtime on the previous run
  • Pending passives — see the $ sitting in your nightclub at day's end
  • Final liquidation — partial sells are valued on the last day

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Why these specific activities?
They're the ones most crime-sim players actually run for money — a mix of heists with cooldowns, passive businesses, and quick fill-in activities. But nothing is hardcoded: the Settings panel lets you edit every number, remove anything you don't use, and add your own.
Where do the default payout numbers come from?
They're community-sourced approximations for a typical solo setup at base difficulty. Your numbers will vary based on crew cuts, difficulty modifiers, build, and rotating bonuses. Overwrite them in Settings to match your own rig — the calculator respects whatever you put in.
How is "effective $/hr" calculated?
Total earned divided by total active play time across all days. It accounts for cooldown waits, passive accrual, setup downtime, and the final-day partial sell of any passive stock sitting in the till.
Does it save my settings?
Yes — activities, payouts, and preferences are stored in localStorage on your device. Nothing is sent to a server. Clear site data to reset.
Is this affiliated with Rockstar or Take-Two?
No. This is an independent, unofficial fan-made tool. No game assets, logos, or trademarks are used. All trademarks belong to their respective owners.
Can I use it for other games?
Anything with cooldown-gated activities and passive accrual — MMOs, survival games, idle games — should work fine. Wipe the defaults, enter your own, and simulate away.
